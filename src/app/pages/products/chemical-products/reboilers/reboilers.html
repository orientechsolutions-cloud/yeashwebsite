Kettle Type Reboilers

Kettle Reboilers are widely used due to their robust design, ease of maintenance, and consistent thermal performance. They operate by circulating the bottom liquid (commonly known as the column bottoms) through the reboiler, where it is partially vaporized and returned to the column.

How It Works
  • Steam or heating medium flows through the tube bundle, entering as vapor and exiting as condensate.
  • The column bottom liquid flows through the shell side, where it receives heat from the tube bundle.
  • A retaining wall or overflow weir ensures that the tube bundle remains fully submerged, improving heat transfer efficiency.
  • The partially vaporized liquid returns to the distillation column, while the unvaporized liquid (bottoms product) is withdrawn from the shell section.
  • If the column does not provide sufficient liquid head, a pump-assisted circulation system can be incorporated.

This design effectively reduces the presence of low-boiling compounds in the bottoms product and maintains stable operational performance.
